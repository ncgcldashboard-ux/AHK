Learnings from Riyadh ‚Äì Findings & Insights
Strategic Readout
Frontier & Positioning
Breaking into a new frontier
  • Existing SBP guarantee covers are typically embedded, documentation-light, and priced at zero to the bank.
  • NCGCL is introducing commercially priced, risk-based, market-oriented guarantee products with enforceable terms.
  • This requires deliberate market education and re-orientation of incentives.
Market Adoption Challenge
Shifting from ‚Äúfree lunch‚Äù to discipline
  • Transitioning PFIs to paid guarantees requires clarity on value: risk transfer, capital efficiency, and scale unlock.
  • Early iterations are necessarily conservative: pilots, caps, and strict reporting/claims discipline.
  • Progress is slow but steady‚Äîtransactions are now covering multiple product types across PFIs.
What resonated
Simple, repeatable narratives
  • Clear articulation of ‚Äúwhat is covered‚Äù and ‚Äúwhat remains with the bank‚Äù is more persuasive than technical depth.
  • Standardised documentation and monitoring framework increases confidence.
  • Impact quantification (ticket size √ó borrowers) materially improves decision comfort.
Practical takeaway: Keep the pitch anchored on (1) risk ladder clarity, (2) approval layer governance, and (3) quantified impact‚Äîthen attach technical annexes in research dashboards.

Guarantee Explainer ‚Äì Products & Risk Ladder
One Definition Each
Risk ladder (NCGCL loss exposure)
From lowest risk to highest risk
Lowest exposure
MPO (Mezzanine Provisioning Offset)
A provisioning / FSV benefit window that improves a PFI‚Äôs provisioning position on eligible NPL cohorts. It is not a ‚Äúfirst cash-loss‚Äù layer and is treated as non-contingent capital exposure in the portfolio view.
Low‚Äìmoderate exposure
Pari Passu Guarantee
NCGCL shares losses pro-rata with the PFI on an agreed percentage of the portfolio (e.g., 50%). Recoveries are shared pro-rata after claim payout.
Moderate exposure
Second Loss Guarantee
NCGCL absorbs losses only after a defined first-loss layer is exhausted (e.g., SBP FL first). The PFI retains residual losses beyond NCGCL‚Äôs layer.
Highest exposure
First Loss Guarantee
NCGCL absorbs initial losses up to the coverage cap before losses hit the PFI. This is the highest loss-absorption product and is therefore most capital-intensive.
Mapping to current transactions:
  • First loss ‚Äì BIPL LT.
  • Second loss ‚Äì BIPL ST (above SBP first-loss).
  • Pari passu ‚Äì PMIC (and account-level pari passu in HBL MFB PCG component).
  • MPO ‚Äì HBL MFB (FSV benefit window; lowest risk exposure).
What each product ‚Äúmeans‚Äù
Simple definitions (board-safe)
  • First Loss: NCGCL absorbs the first slice of losses up to the agreed cap, enabling a PFI to lend deeper into riskier segments with discipline.
  • Second Loss: NCGCL only pays after a pre-agreed first-loss layer is exhausted (e.g., SBP first loss), ensuring primary de-risking remains with sponsor layer.
  • Pari Passu: NCGCL and the PFI share verified losses pro-rata; preserves ‚Äúskin in the game‚Äù while enabling scale through partial risk transfer.
  • MPO: A mezzanine provisioning relief mechanism (FSV benefit) that supports portfolio clean-up / risk appetite without behaving like a first-cash-loss layer.
Commercialisation note: Unlike free, embedded guarantee schemes, NCGCL products require enforceable documentation, reporting, and claims discipline‚Äîthis is the core market transition.

  • Approve BIPL ST (Agri Input) portfolio of PKR 1.0bn with NCGCL providing 10% second-loss cover (PKR 100m cap) above SBP‚Äôs first-loss layer, subject to RAC, reporting and claims protocol.
  • Approve BIPL LT (Agri & Productive Assets) pilot portfolio of ~PKR 660m with NCGCL providing 10% first-loss cover (PKR 100m cap), with scale-up provisions subject to pilot performance and policy thresholds.
  • Approve UNIDO Sindh Consortium pilot facility of PKR 200m with NCGCL providing 40% portfolio cover (PKR 80m cap), subject to governance, eligibility filters and monitoring framework.
  • Approve HBL Microfinance Bank facility comprising a 25% account-level pari passu PCG layer capped at PKR 1.0bn (contingent exposure), and an MPO window of up to PKR 500m (FSV benefit; non-contingent), subject to covenants and reporting/claims discipline.
For Board-level approval (wording requirement)
  • Recommend for onward approval to the Board for the PMIC Portfolio Credit Guarantee facility of PKR 2.0bn with NCGCL providing 50% pari passu guarantee of principal losses up to PKR 1.0bn, noting related-party status and applicable governance requirements.
Rule reference: Board approval required where Max Contingent Exposure > PKR 1.0bn and/or for related-party transactions (PMIC).
