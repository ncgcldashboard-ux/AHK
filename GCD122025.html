BIPL ‚Äì Smallholder Agri Input Finance (6-Month Cycle)
Action: Approve 10% Second-Loss Pilot
Product Type
Seasonal smallholder agri input portfolio (Islamic structure)
Guarantee Cover
10% NCGCL second-loss (over 10% SBP first-loss)
NCGCL Exposure
PKR 100m cap on PKR 1.0bn portfolio
Facility Size
PKR 1.0bn
  • Revolving finance for inputs (fertilizer, seed, pesticides etc.).
  • Self-liquidating at harvest; 6-month bullet loans.
Guarantee Structure
  • SBP: 10% first-loss on the portfolio.
  • NCGCL: additional 10% second-loss above SBP‚Äôs layer.
  • BIPL: retains remaining 80% of ultimate loss.
Tenor & Pricing
  • Tenor: up to 6 months.
  • Guarantee fee: 1.0% flat, charged at maturity.
  • Pricing aligned with approved Shariah-compliant grid.
Eligibility & Portfolio Criteria
  • Smallholders up to 12.5 acres; KYC and CNIC verified.
  • Internal and ECIB checks with no severe delinquency history.
  • Diversified by crop and geography; caps on single-borrower and group exposure.
Controls, Reporting & Claims
  • Mandatory Takaful; standardized documents and digital MIS.
  • Monthly reporting on disbursements, PAR (Portfolio At Risk) and recoveries.
  • Claims admissible only after SBP‚Äôs 10% first-loss layer is fully exhausted and recoveries documented.
Key Risks & Mitigants
  • Weather / yield risk: short tenor, geographic diversification, insurance.
  • Moral hazard: high residual exposure for BIPL; hard PAR trip-wires and RAC compliance.
  • Operational risk: standardized processes, audit rights, independent monitoring.
Decision Sought: Approve PKR 1.0bn smallholder agri input portfolio with NCGCL providing 10% second-loss cover (PKR 100m cap) over SBP‚Äôs 10% first-loss, subject to RAC and PAR (Portfolio At Risk) triggers.

Key Risks & Mitigants

  • NCGCL exposure capped; structured as first-loss to crowd-in BIPL capital.
  • Pilot learnings to inform eventual scale-up or recalibration.

HBL Microfinance Bank ‚Äì Micro & Agri PCG + MPO Window
Action: Approve 25% Pari Passu PCG & MPO Facility
Product Type
Tiered portfolio credit guarantee and mezzanine provisioning offset on IFC-backed micro & agri portfolio.
Guarantee Cover
25% account-level pari passu guarantee (incremental lending) + MPO FSV benefit window.
NCGCL Exposure
PPG cap PKR 1.0bn + MPO cap PKR 500m (aggregate PKR 1.5bn).
Purpose & Facility Size
PKR 1.5bn allocation
  • Provide partial risk coverage to HBL MFB for lending to micro borrowers, microenterprises and smallholder farmers.
  • Anchored on existing IFC 50% risk participation to crowd-in additional private capital.
  • Allocation split between 25% PPG layer and MPO FSV benefit on NPL portfolio.
Guarantee Structure
  • IFC already provides 50% account-level coverage.
  • NCGCL adds 25% pari passu layer: aggregate cover up to 75% at account level.
  • NCGCL PPG kicks in once IFC has honored claims up to 25% at portfolio level.
  • MPO: up to PKR 500m FSV benefit on eligible NPL portfolio; no direct credit coverage but provisioning relief.
Tenor & Pricing
  • Tenor: up to 3 years, aligned with underlying micro and agri cycles.
  • PPG fee: 1.50% p.a. on guaranteed portion of average outstanding principal, payable quarterly in arrears.
  • MPO fee: 1.00% p.a. on average FSV benefit utilized, payable quarterly in arrears.
Eligibility & Covenants
  • Eligible portfolios: micro, microenterprise and smallholder farmer financings; housing exposures excluded as per mandate.
  • Overall portfolio infection ratio covenant: ‚â§ 8%; breach triggers portfolio freeze and joint review.
  • Minimum Capital Adequacy Ratio: ‚â• 12% throughout facility life.
  • Facility governed by NCGCL Guarantee Policy 2025 and RAC, including sector and geographic caps.
Monitoring, Reporting & Claims
  • Monthly portfolio data submissions: utilisation, PAR (Portfolio At Risk), NPLs, provisions and MPO FSV usage.
  • Quarterly joint review meetings to assess data integrity, covenant compliance, concentration and remedial actions.
  • Claims admissible post verified-loss condition and exhaustion of internal recoveries by HBL MFB and IFC processes.
  • Standardised documentation: loan files, recovery evidence and loss confirmations to accompany each claim.
Key Risks & Mitigants
  • Portfolio credit risk: mitigated through layered loss absorption (IFC 50%, NCGCL 25%) and hard PAR/NPL covenants.
  • Operational / reporting risk: controlled via monthly data feeds, standard templates and joint audit rights.
  • Liquidity & funding risk at HBL MFB: offset by strong sponsor support (HBL, AKAM), comfortable CAR and leverage profile.
  • Regulatory and conversion risk: managed under SBP oversight and HBL group Islamic conversion roadmap.
Decision Sought: Approve HBL Microfinance Bank portfolio credit guarantee facility comprising:
  • A 25% account-level pari passu guarantee layer, capped at PKR 1.0bn, complementing IFC‚Äôs 50% participation; and
  • A Mezzanine Provisioning Offset (MPO) window of up to PKR 500m FSV benefit on eligible NPL portfolio,
subject to compliance with Guarantee Policy 2025, RAC criteria, covenants (infection ratio ‚â§ 8%, CAR ‚â• 12%) and standard claims and monitoring protocols.

Action: Approve Blended PCG Pilot
Product Type
Blended finance PCG for micro & small enterprises in Sindh
Guarantee Cover
40% NCGCL portfolio guarantee
NCGCL Exposure
PKR 80m cap on PKR 200m consortium facility
Facility Size
PKR 200m
  • Consortium facility combining UNIDO grant support, PBIC lines and MFI lending.
  • Focus on micro & small enterprises in targeted Sindh districts.
Guarantee Structure
  • NCGCL provides a 40% portfolio guarantee over eligible loans.
  • Total NCGCL exposure capped at PKR 80m.
  • Grant support from UNIDO further de-risks underlying projects.
  • Risk-sharing alongside PBIC and participating MFIs.

  • Micro and small enterprises with emphasis on:
    • women-led businesses,
    • youth-led businesses, and
    • transgender-led businesses.
  • Target districts include Tharparkar, Thatta, Sujawal, Badin and Larkana.
  • Aligned with UNIDO‚Äôs programme objectives and sector focus.

  • Clear roles for UNIDO, PBIC, SAFCO, TMF and NCGCL under a consortium framework.

  • Quarterly consortium reviews to track KPIs, impact and risk metrics.
Risk Management
  • Group- and community-based lending and social collateral where appropriate.
  • Portfolio PAR (Portfolio At Risk) triggers with ability to rebalance, tighten criteria or pause disbursements.
  • NCGCL exposure ring-fenced to a defined pilot pool, with explicit exit and scale-up criteria.

  • Recoveries shared according to agreed waterfall between PFIs, UNIDO-related funds (where applicable) and NCGCL.

Consolidated View
  • Institutional diversification
    • Diversified across BIPL (Islamic bank), PMIC (apex microfinance wholesaler), HBL Microfinance Bank (largest MFB) and a blended consortium (UNIDO/PBIC/SAFCO/TMF).

  • Risk-sharing architecture
    • Layered first-loss, second-loss, pari passu and mezzanine structures ensure PFIs retain majority risk.
    • Operational responsibility and primary recoveries remain with PFIs.
  • Self-liquidating guarantees
    • All guarantees are self-liquidating, with tenors matched to underlying assets.
    • Clear, time-bound claim procedures and recovery waterfalls reduce tail risk.
  • Harmonised monitoring & early intervention
    • Harmonised RACs, reporting templates and claim protocols across agri, microfinance and blended portfolios.
    • Enables consolidated monitoring, early warning via PAR (Portfolio At Risk) / NPL metrics and swift intervention.
